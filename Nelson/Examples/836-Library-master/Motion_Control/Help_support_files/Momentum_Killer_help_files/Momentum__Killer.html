Momentum_Killer.vi

Help by FRC Team 836 - The RoboBees

This VI operates as a motor brake to bring mechanical motion to a quicker stop than usual. When commanded by KILL CONTROL, the POWER OUTPUT

is reversed for a short time to counter the momentum of the motor and it's rotating group. It is similar to using the passive shorting brake on a motor

controller, except that this is a must faster active brake controlled by the setting of REVERSE POWER and REVERSE TIME.

A state machine is incorporated to manage the operating states of the VI. Initial state is 0/Normal state, where the POWER OUTPUT is equal to the POWER INPUT.

Upon the KILL CONTROL command, the 1/Reverse state begins where POWER OUTPUT is equal to REVERSE POWER for REVERSE TIME in seconds. At the end

of the reverse time period, the 2/Stop state provides a zero to POWER OUTPUT for STOP DELAY time in seconds, before returning to 0/Normal state.

The EDGE input is used to modify the behavior of KILL CONTROL. A true EDGE will initiate the braking process on the rising edge of KILL CONTROL.

When EDGE is false, the falling edge of KILL CONTROL initiates.

Tuning is accomplished by selecting a short REVERSE TIME (generally part of a second), and experimentally finding the proper value of REVERSE POWER that

brings mechanical motion to an abrupt halt (usually without actually reversing motion). If motion cannot be halted at maximum reverse power, increase

REVERSE TIME. Several combinations of solutions for reverse time and power will stop the motion, but one generally wants to find the shortest time to do so.

Doesn't work on Spike Relays!
